# Texts

Reading notes, annotations, and summaries organized by author or topic cluster.

## How to Use This Section

- Add a sub-page per text or author
- Use the Philosopher agent to generate structured reading notes
- Link argument entries back to their source texts with `[[arguments/...]]`

## Primary Text Template

```markdown
---
title: Author — Work Title (Year)
tags: [author-name, topic, period]
---

## Bibliographic Info
- Full citation
- Edition / translation used (if relevant)

## Central Claims
What the text is fundamentally arguing.

## Key Arguments
Brief reconstruction of the main argumentative moves.

## Difficult Passages
Quotes and interpretations of passages that are unclear or contested.

## Our Reading
Where we agree, disagree, or are uncertain.

## Connection to Our Work
How this text bears on our current projects.
```

## Suggested Organization

- `texts/canonical/` — foundational historical texts
- `texts/contemporary/` — recent secondary literature and debates
- `texts/adjacent/` — relevant work from adjacent fields (cognitive science, linguistics, social theory, etc.)
