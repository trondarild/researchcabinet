# Example Philosophy Lab

A sample Cabinet for a theory and philosophy research group. The knowledge base is organized around arguments, texts, projects, and seminars — the natural units of philosophical work.

## What Makes This Different

Philosophy research doesn't accumulate data points — it accumulates positions, objections, distinctions, and arguments. This Cabinet is structured to track those explicitly alongside the primary and secondary literature.

## Lab Modules

- [[arguments]] — Active argument maps, positions, and objection tracking
- [[texts]] — Reading notes on primary and secondary sources
- [[projects]] — Papers, chapters, and longer research projects in progress
- [[seminars]] — Seminar reading lists, prep notes, and session summaries
- [[lab]] — Team, goals, and lab operations

## Active AI Team

- Research Director — philosophical strategy, project prioritization, seminar planning
- Philosopher — primary and secondary literature, argument reconstruction, hypothesis generation
- Argument Analyst — maps positions, tracks objections, identifies dialectical structure

The example agents are paused by default. Activate them in the Agents panel and rewrite their personas to match your group's focus — analytic, continental, history of philosophy, political theory, philosophy of science, etc.
